Introduction

Our ultimate goal in Softer Shock is to protect vineyards from extreme temperature events by applying a microorganism on their leaves. This apparently simple task actually faces a great number of difficulties and needs to integrate different sciences to obtain a concrete and efficient product. The goal of this part is to show you what was our reasoning with such a project and make you understand all of its facets. Here you will find various parts that are all completed by a report available for all. The final and last report will integrate all the others and present you the final product. Have fun !

Working with the plant: perspectives for chassis selection in accordance with the phyllosphere

For our project, we want a specific chassis for a great number of reasons. The leaf ecosystem is a harsh environment on which a great number of organisms live and survive ! They have awesome adaptive properties and their population is shaped by a lot of dynamics. Our goal here is to understand this environment and its inhabitants to predict the best way how our microorganisms will behave once applied. We thought that selecting a chassis based on the leaf surface microbiota called the phyllosphere, could help into improving efficiency and safety of Softer Shock ! Find out everything you need about the incredible phyllosphere and our chassis selection strategy in the next report !

Compound choice : How to protect the plant against extreme temperature events

Frost and desiccation are our enemies, and against them we have a great number of weapons ! To fight both, different strategies have been offered to us, from antifreeze proteins to sunlight reflecting compounds. We based ourselves on the complex grapevine physiology in response to meteorological events, but also on previous concrete cases of attempts to fight the resulting damages. Biosafety : Killswitch and contamination-limiting diffusion

We are going to spread genetically modified organisms on crops in an open environment. This idea itself makes our project difficult and exposed to many objections. While we are aware of the risks Softer Shock implies, we tried our best to maximise the biosafety of our project through a great number of strategies, from synthetic auxotrophy to harvesting panels.
